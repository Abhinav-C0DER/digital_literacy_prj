# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: Essential Checklist for College Students

This checklist provides actionable steps to protect your digital identity and financial assets while navigating the web.

### **General Digital Hygiene**
- [ ] **Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Use app-based authenticators (like Google Authenticator) rather than SMS for all academic and social media accounts.
- [ ] **Secure Your System:** Since you are likely using Linux or Windows, ensure your kernel and applications are updated regularly to patch security vulnerabilities (e.g., `sudo pacman -Syu`).
- [ ] **Practice Link Skepticism:** Avoid clicking on shortened URLs in WhatsApp groups or "too-good-to-be-true" internship offers sent via DM without verifying the source.
- [ ] **Use a Password Manager:** Stop reusing the same password across platforms. Use a tool like Bitwarden or KeepassXC to manage unique, complex passwords.
- [ ] **Audit Social Media Privacy:** Limit who can see your contact details and "check-ins" on Instagram and LinkedIn to prevent social engineering attacks.
- [ ] **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Never log into your bank account or student portal using open campus or cafe Wi-Fi without a trusted VPN.

### **💸 Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)**
- [ ] **PIN is Only for Sending Money:** Remember that you **never** need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to *receive* money. If someone asks you to enter a PIN to receive a "scholarship" or "refund," it is a scam.
- [ ] **Verify UPI IDs and Names:** Always double-check the verified name displayed on the UPI app before hitting 'Pay.' Scammers often use names similar to official organizations to deceive students.

---

### **🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India**

If you or someone you know has been a victim of a digital or financial fraud, report it immediately:

1.  **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
2.  **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Call immediately for financial frauds to increase the chances of freezing the stolen funds).

*Stay vigilant, stay secure.*
