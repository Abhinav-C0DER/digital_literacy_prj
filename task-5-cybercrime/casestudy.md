# Case Study: Business Email Compromise (BEC)

### **The Crime**
Business Email Compromise (BEC) is a sophisticated form of cyber-enabled fraud where an attacker targets an organization to facilitate an unauthorized transfer of funds. Unlike traditional phishing, which uses broad "bait," BEC is a surgical strike that relies on social engineering and identity theft to deceive employees into believing they are interacting with a trusted colleague or vendor.

### **The Process (Step-by-Step)**
1.  **Reconnaissance:** The attacker spends weeks researching an organization’s structure and vendor relationships using public data from LinkedIn, company websites, or leaked databases.
2.  **Impersonation:** The criminal registers a "look-alike" domain (e.g., using `billing@vender.com` instead of `vendor.com`) or gains unauthorized access to a legitimate executive’s email account.
3.  **The Hook:** An "urgent" email is sent to the finance department, claiming a long-term partner has changed their banking details and requires immediate payment for an overdue invoice.
4.  **The Execution:** Under the perceived pressure of a high-ranking executive or a faked deadline, the employee bypasses standard verification protocols and wires the funds directly to the attacker’s offshore account.

### **Target Demographics**
The primary targets are mid-to-senior level employees with wire-transfer authority, such as CFOs, accountants, or supply chain managers. Organizations that frequently perform international wire transfers or deal with high-volume hardware procurement are especially high-risk.

### **Consequences**
The consequences are often catastrophic. Financially, BEC scams account for billions in global losses annually. Beyond the immediate loss of capital, the company suffers significant reputational damage and potential legal action. For the individual employee, the result is often severe disciplinary action or termination, serving as a harsh reminder that human vulnerability is the most common entry point for modern cybercriminals.
