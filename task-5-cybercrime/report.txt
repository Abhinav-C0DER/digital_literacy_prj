The most surprising discovery during my research was the sheer psychological precision behind Business Email Compromise (BEC) and local UPI scams. I previously assumed that cybercrime relied almost entirely on sophisticated technical exploits or software vulnerabilities. However, learning how attackers spend weeks performing reconnaissance to exploit human trust—often tricking victims into scanning QR codes to "receive" money—was a striking revelation. It proved that the "human firewall" is frequently the most targeted and vulnerable link in any security chain.

As a direct result, I am changing my habit of treating mobile transactions and digital requests as routine, low-risk actions. I have implemented a strict "zero-trust" policy for any unsolicited payment links or QR codes. Additionally, I am migrating my accounts from SMS-based verification to app-based Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) to mitigate SIM-swapping risks. This research has shifted my perspective: staying safe is less about the tools I use and more about maintaining a constant state of digital mindfulness.
