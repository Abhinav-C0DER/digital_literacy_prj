-----------------------------------------------------------
RESPONSIBLE SOCIAL MEDIA USE: CHECKLIST FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS
-----------------------------------------------------------

THE DO'S:
1. DO use professional platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub to showcase your technical projects and academic milestones.
2. DO regularly audit your privacy settings to control who can see your personal data and tagged photos.
3. DO verify the credibility of a source before sharing news or technical information to avoid spreading misinformation.
4. DO engage respectfully with industry leaders and peers to build a meaningful and supportive professional network.
5. DO curate your profile as a "digital resume," knowing that recruiters will likely search for your name.

THE DON'TS:
1. DON'T post unprofessional content, such as public complaints about professors, peers, or past employers.
2. DON'T overshare your real-time location or sensitive personal data that could compromise your privacy or safety.
3. DON'T engage in toxic online arguments or "flame wars" that can negatively impact your mental health and digital reputation.
4. DON'T plagiarize content or claim credit for open-source code and projects that are not your own.
5. DON'T post or stay tagged in content that depicts illegal activities or violations of your university’s code of conduct.
